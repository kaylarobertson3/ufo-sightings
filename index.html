Is it a bird? Or a plane? If it isn’t not clear, it’s an unidentified flying object. Between 1910 and 2013, there were over 65,000 reports* of Unidentified Flying Object sightings in the United States, according to the National UFO Reporting Center (NURC), an organization that collects and investigates UFO sightings.


Despite reports that UFO sightings are dwindling, the U.S. government hasn't stopped from dedicating funds and research to the phenomenon. The Pentagon's top-secret UFO research office, the Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program began in 2007 and ended in 2012, but not before going through $22 million in funding. It came to public attention following a New York Times investigation into the organization in 2017. While the Defense Intelligence Agency has since released 38 research titles about their findings, part of the data remains classified.


The once-secret organization isn't the only way the United States shows interest in extraterrestrial research. In February 2019, President Trump signed into effect the U.S. Space Force, a national security entity and space warfare service. While the Space Force isn't primarily for UFO research, it's the first time the military has had a dedicated space sector in the United States, continuing a history of the United States' government taking interest in matters beyond our atmosphere. Two months later, the U.S. Navy announced that they've had an increase of UFO sightings, and drafted new guidelines for officially reporting UFOs, Politico reports. While this doesn't mean the Navy is suddenly concerned about aliens, it puts into place a more formal reporting process so no unknown sightings slip through the cracks, a former Pentagon intelligence officer told Politico.


"There have been a number of reports of unauthorized and/or unidentified aircraft entering various military-controlled ranges and designated air space in recent years," the Navy said in response to questions from POLITICO. "For safety and security concerns, the Navy and the [U.S. Air Force] takes these reports very seriously and investigates each and every report." As the U.S prepares for shifts in the way space and UFOs are studied, scroll down to look back at nearly 100 years of NUFORC UFO reports to understand what they tell us about what we see in the night sky.

UFO sightings by weekday and time

Of the 65,114 UFO sightings in the US between January 1910 and December 2013, unsurprisingly, not many sightings happen when the sun is out. Most sightings happen at night, when the mysterious objects’ lights can be seen. Hover over a circle to find out how many UFO sightings occurred on a certain weekday and time.

Late, weekend nights

Most sightings happen around the end of the workweek, between 19:00 and 23:00. Many reports mention “stepping outside for a cigarette”, while others describe spotting UFOS while inside a vehicle. The most common day for UFO spotting is Saturday, with Friday at a close second. The best time for UFO sightings is 9pm or 10pm.

West is best

Out of the total number of UFO sightings per state (normalized by population), many happen in the West or North East. Hover or click on a state to see how many UFO sightings occurred per 100,000 people between 1910 and 2013.

Washington

The highest number of UFO sightings was in Washington at 58 sightings per 100,000 people. Hover over a circle to see details about each reported incident.

Montana

Montana also boasts a high sighting rate by population, possibly due to its large size and many rural, wooded areas. Hover over a circle to see details about each reported incident.

Circles, triangles and fireballs

According to NURC reports, 21.2 percent of UFO sightings are described as looking like "light". 13.47 percent appear as "round" or "circular", and 10.63 percent appear triangle-like.

UFO sightings by month

Most UFO sightings happen in summer and fall, possibly due to spending more time outside. The month that holds the most UFO sightings is July.

Aliens or fireworks?

Out of 7,639 total July sightings from 1910 to 2013, 1,083 happened on July 4th. Whether this is due to people misinterpreting fireworks for UFOS, or simply more people outside at night, looking at the sky, July 4th stands as the most common day to spot a UFO.
